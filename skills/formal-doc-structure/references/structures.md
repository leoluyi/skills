# Document structures

Pick one template by reader need. Adapt it — do not force every document into a project-plan shape. Section names are starting points; merge or rename to fit the case.

## A. Decision or approval document

For sign-off, procurement notes, management approval, or budget requests.

1. 主旨或目的
2. 背景說明
3. 現況與需求
4. 方案或處理方式
5. 風險與控管
6. 經費、人力或資源需求
7. 預期效益或必要性
8. 擬辦事項

## B. Evaluation or assessment report

For risk reviews, vendor evaluations, technology assessments, or option comparisons.

1. 評估目的
2. 評估範圍
3. 評估基準
4. 現況或資料來源
5. 分析結果
6. 風險與限制
7. 建議方案
8. 後續追蹤事項

## C. Meeting record

For internal meetings, vendor meetings, review meetings, and project discussions.

1. 會議資訊
2. 會議目的
3. 討論重點
4. 決議事項
5. 待辦事項
6. 風險或待確認事項
7. 下次追蹤

## D. Implementation or operating plan

For project execution, organizational design, training, process rollout, or operational setup.

1. 文件定位與前提
2. 整體執行模式
3. 角色與分工
4. 時程與階段
5. 工作內容或作業流程
6. 交付物與驗收
7. 風險與控管
8. 後續維運或追蹤

## E. Vendor or external communication

For vendor inquiries, clarification requests, security responses, or cooperation notes.

1. 背景
2. 本方立場或需求
3. 請對方確認事項
4. 需提供資料
5. 時程或回覆方式
6. 後續聯繫窗口

## Structural habits (apply to every template)

### Keep chapters concentrated

Group related topics instead of scattering them. Use fewer, stronger chapters rather than many thin ones.

- 背景 + 現況 often belong together.
- 範圍 + 假設 together.
- 廠商需求 + 交付物 + 驗收標準 together.
- 風險 + 控管 together.
- 評估基準 + 評分方式 together.
- 權責 + 後續追蹤 together.

### Summary before detail

When a section carries heavy analysis, open with one or two sentences of planning intent, conclusion, or operating logic — then the steps, tables, or work items.

> 本案預計以分階段方式執行，前期完成基礎資料盤點與需求確認，中期進行方案設計與驗證，後期依驗收結果納入正式作業流程。

### Don't repeat other documents

In an appendix, supporting note, or follow-up, do not restate content defined elsewhere. Reference the parent, then focus on this document's unique purpose:

- 本文件承接主文件之……
- 相關條件以附加文件為準……
- 本文件聚焦……，不重複說明……

## Table usage

### Use tables only where they improve clarity

Good for: comparison; responsibility split; timeline / phases; role-to-task mapping; unit-to-work mapping; risk and controls; deliverables and acceptance criteria; vendor scope; evaluation criteria; payment milestones; follow-up tracking.

Avoid tables for narrative that reads better as short paragraphs or bullets.

### Keep tables decision-oriented

Each table should answer a practical question: Who does what? When? What is the scope? What must be delivered? How is it evaluated? Which unit is responsible? What risk exists and how is it controlled? What must the vendor provide?

If a table only repeats the surrounding text, remove it.

### Do not over-fragment

Prefer one complete table over several small overlapping ones. If two tables share columns and purpose, merge them — unless the merge makes the table too wide to read.
